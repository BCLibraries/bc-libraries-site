Dear Member of the Boston College Community,

I am writing today to tell you about a new project underway in the Boston College Libraries. Beginning January 1st, members of the Boston College community have access to a one-year long trial of Scopus. Scopus is a multidisciplinary database that provides keyword, author and affiliation searching, along with journal analysis. While similar in many ways to the Web of Science database, it is larger and offers an easy, intuitive interface for author and affiliation searching. Each database's subject coverage varies by discipline and even sub-discipline.  Both databases can be accessed from the Boston College Libraries' Databases page.

Both databases offer:

  • Wide-ranging exploratory research searching
  • Identification of potential collaborators at Boston College and beyond
  • Scholarly productivity analysis, such as H-Index analysis  
  • Journal "impact" comparison tools, which can be useful in identifying potential journal publication targets
  • Ability to discover funding sources

We ask your help with the goal of identifying the best resources for Boston College.   We request that you use each database in your research and teaching, and tell us what you think, by providing feedback in a survey later this semester and, for a small number of you, in focus groups.  We will be offering ample training opportunities for all. We'll also be offering a graduate student stipend for a small group of students to work with us on research projects in their fields, comparing results from each database and how each serves their needs.

Again, please take this opportunity to explore both of these important tools; having your feedback will be critical to our making a wise decision for future access.

If you have any questions or feedback, please don't hesitate to contact your subject specialist, or email the members of the task force charged with this investigation (scopustf-ggroup@bc.edu); they have set up a Scopus Feedback form, which I would encourage you to use.

With my best wishes for a good semester,

Thomas B. Wall
University Librarian
